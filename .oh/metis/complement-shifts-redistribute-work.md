title: "Complement shifts redistribute work"

---

A complement shift is a change in the division of labor, not an argument for
keeping or restoring every role. The useful explanation names what automation
now does, what contribution becomes more valuable or newly necessary, where it
enters the work, and which outcome shows whether the pairing works.

Ford made this concrete: automation scans, tests, and flags at scale while
veteran engineers bring failure knowledge, judgment, teaching, and feedback
upstream. CBA shows what happens when a company removes a role before tracing
those contributions. Salesforce shows expertise moving into evaluation,
operations, adoption, and product feedback. Block keeps genuine substitution
open as a possible result rather than assuming every cut must fail.

This matters because listing people, process, technology, and authority does
not explain a mechanism. Leaders need to show how each contribution changes
the outcome and revise that explanation as real work produces evidence.
