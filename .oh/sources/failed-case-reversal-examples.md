# Source Excerpt: Failed Cases as Reversal Examples

These examples should make it into the essay set as relatable evidence of the reversal mechanism. They are supporting examples, not the main `strategy-report.md` chart. The main chart remains the reversal stack plus the mechanism/literature/how-it-shows-up table.

## Core claim to carry

Failed cases often reveal the logic of reversal in its strongest form: the system becomes better at staging compliance than at achieving the underlying objective.

- Volkswagen optimized the emissions test rather than emissions reality.
- Wells Fargo optimized cross-sell counts rather than customer value.
- Boeing optimized schedule and program continuity while its formal safety system lost real veto power.

Use these with references and enough factual grounding that readers can map the pattern to their own organizations: KPI optimization, audit-performance, schedule commitment, dashboard compliance, and formal review channels that lose causal authority.

## Cases to spell out

| Case | Reversal pattern | Evidence to cite | Essay use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Volkswagen diesel emissions** | Compliance reversed into performance-for-the-test. The system recognized the audit condition and optimized the measured ceremony while violating emissions reality. | EPA Notice of Violation says VW used software that detected when vehicles were undergoing emissions testing and switched controls on during testing, while emissions controls were reduced in normal operation; the notice states NOx emissions could be up to 40 times the standard in normal driving. | Use in the communication reversal essay beside auditability: when auditability becomes the governing medium, work can optimize for what the audit recognizes instead of the underlying objective. |
| **Wells Fargo sales practices** | Customer value reversed into metric production. Cross-sell targets became load-bearing, so the organization optimized counts while the customer reality was violated. | CFPB announced a $100 million penalty for the widespread illegal practice of secretly opening unauthorized accounts; OCC and SEC materials are useful for sales-practice and disclosure context. | Use in the work-to-strategy feedback or reversal essay: evidence existed, but the incentive/control surface made the metric outrank customer reality. |
| **Boeing 737 MAX** | Safety governance reversed into program-continuity support. Formal safety systems existed, but schedule, cost, competitive pressure, and delegated review channels weakened real veto power. | The House Transportation Committee report describes production pressure, faulty assumptions, and a culture of concealment; DOT OIG reported weaknesses in FAA certification and delegation processes. | Use in the reversal essay as the high-stakes example of formal review losing causal authority: the existence of safety process is not the same as safety evidence having veto power. |

## Carry-forward note

These examples should be spelled out with enough evidence that a reader does not need to already know the scandals. The point is not scandal recap. The point is recognition: every organization has smaller versions of this pattern when measurement, auditability, schedule, governance, or reporting becomes more consequential than the underlying objective.
