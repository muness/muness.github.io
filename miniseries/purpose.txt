Purpose

Ideal reader:
- Engineering leaders, staff/principal engineers, and product/ops leads who feel AI increased output but not coherence.
- Teams stuck in “pilot purgatory,” tool debates, or delivery-path drag who want a repeatable system, not a new tool.
- Readers who like the Phoenix Project / Lencioni style: story-driven lessons with concrete moves.

Core takeaway:
- Speed isn’t the constraint; alignment, verification, and durable learning are.
- Open Horizons is a system for making teams collectively right: Aim → Do → Reflect, then terraform the environment so good work becomes the default.
- Use agents to widen options, but use guardrails, review, dissent, and salvage to keep the search governed and the learning compounding.
