# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Serious leaders, operators, product people, and technical teams with a consequential workflow that is slow, inconsistent, expensive, or trapped in a few people's heads. They are evaluating whether Muness can help them change the work, not merely add an AI tool. Receptive collaborators and unusually high-trust operating roles are a secondary audience.

## Product Purpose

Muness.com makes one coherent body of work legible: consulting that changes workflows, public tools and experiments that expose the method, and writing that explains the mechanisms. The primary outcome is a context-rich inquiry from a good-fit team. Success means a visitor understands the proposition and evidence quickly, can judge fit without a sales call, and knows how to start.

## Brand Personality

Calm, technical, and opinionated. The site should feel like direct contact with an experienced builder-operator: precise enough to inspect, human enough to disagree with, and confident without hype.

## Anti-references

- A stock personal blog with consulting added to the copy.
- A generic AI consultancy or SaaS landing page: gradients, mythology, inflated metrics, empty claims, and interchangeable cards.
- An editorial-magazine performance that makes the typography more important than the work.
- A project catalog in which every repository competes equally for attention.
- Client proof that compromises confidentiality or invents outcomes.

## Design Principles

- Start with the consequential workflow and the person who owns it.
- Show how judgment enters the system; never imply that automation owns the decision.
- Make evidence inspectable. Public artifacts should do more work than adjectives.
- Give every page and project one clear role in the story.
- Use selectivity as useful guidance: help good-fit people act and poor-fit people opt out.

## Design Practice

Frontend design work uses the `impeccable` skill family for shaping, critique, audit, refinement, and visual verification. Treat this file as the design brief and the shipped interface as the existing design system. Preserve established identity before applying new skill defaults.

The site should feel deliberately made for this body of work, not assembled from current landing-page conventions. Design decisions must strengthen hierarchy, comprehension, credibility, and fit. Verify meaningful interface changes in the browser across relevant viewport sizes before shipping.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast, keyboard access, focus visibility, semantic structure, and target sizing. The responsive site must remain legible at narrow widths and with enlarged text. Motion is optional, never required for comprehension, and must respect reduced-motion preferences.
