# I build systems and teams that can change.

Work can get stuck behind a missing decision, a rule no one can explain, or dependence on one person. I have seen the same pattern in software and in teams.

I trace what actually happens, identify the decisions and rules inside it, and make a change concrete enough to try. In software, that may mean running a case the team understands. In a team, it may mean changing who can decide or what evidence they can see.

The people closest to the work should be able to show what is wrong and correct it. They should be able to keep improving the system or the way the team works without waiting for me. Otherwise I have left them another dependency.

## What this is in service of

This site is one place I further three aims:

- **Choose where I invest.** Give deeply where there is trust, action, reciprocity, and a chance the work gets carried forward. Stop treating every failure of reception as a mandate to explain harder.
- **Build agent work that changes behavior.** Use consulting, tools, writing, and weird-but-real projects to help serious people change how they work with agents, with more judgment rather than less.
- **Use margin, don't just protect it.** Leave room for family, grief, walking, cooking, and things I want to make for no better reason than they should exist.

## Outside client work

I keep my consulting commitments limited. I want time with my family, and I want room to build things that may never become client work—like the physical knob on my couch that controls four audio systems in the basement. It works. I use it every day.
