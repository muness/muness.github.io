I was a programmer before I was an executive. I spent more than fifteen years building web applications, coaching developers, and leading engineering teams. Then I focused on data and led data organizations at JPMC, Zapier, and Shopify.

After NerdWallet, I thought the next move was another engineering or data leadership role. That was the frame behind the old homepage. It is no longer a good description of the work.

I am back in the code now. I consult with teams putting agents into real work, build open-source tools and methods, run experiments, and write about what LLMs change in software and organizations.

Leading those organizations changed what I look for when I build. A technically plausible system can fail because nobody owns the decision, the team cannot replay a bad result, or the metric rewards usage instead of effectiveness. I look for those failures before treating the code as finished.

## What connects the work

The medium changes. The questions do not:

- What should a person or team be able to do differently?
- What part of the system is actually constraining that change?
- Where does judgment belong?
- What evidence says the work is good enough?
- Who owns the result after the model, consultant, or executive leaves the room?

That is what connects consulting, Repo-Native Alignment, Open Horizons Skills, Sketch-CE, the hi-fi control plane, and the writing here.
