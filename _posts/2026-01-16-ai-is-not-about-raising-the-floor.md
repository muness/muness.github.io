## Hook (2–3 sentences)

Most people are using AI to get “good enough” faster: write the email, summarize the doc, produce a first draft. That raises the floor. The bigger shift is that the ceiling moved: individuals and small teams can attempt things that used to require specialists, months, and political capital—*if* they learn to steer, verify, and constrain the work.

## Who This Is For

- Leaders who are equating “AI adoption” with “more output.”
- Builders who feel the ceiling move, but also feel the chaos dragon.
- Teams that ship faster, but also reverse faster.

## Core Claim

AI’s highest leverage is not “typing acceleration.” It’s **reach**: the ability to explore, prototype, and ship in regions of the solution space you previously couldn’t afford to enter.

## Outline

### 1) Floor vs. Ceiling

- Raising the floor: making mediocre work cheaper, faster, and more available.
- Blowing up the ceiling: expanding what you can attempt (and finish) with the same human bandwidth.

### 2) Why Ceiling Gains Don’t Come From “More Model”

- Generation got cheap. Verification didn’t.
- Options got abundant. Judgment became the bottleneck.

### 3) The New Job: Editor, Not Crafter

- Your job is not to write the thing; your job is to choose which things deserve depth.
- Use “Shallow → Score → Select → Deepen” as the default loop (link to {% post_url 2025-12-10-beyond-the-nearest-peak %}).

### 4) The Failure Mode: Thrash At Speed

- Ceiling reach without constraints produces reversals.
- “Polished-but-wrong” becomes common when direction is unconstrained (link to {% post_url 2026-01-12-taming-the-chaos-dragon %}).

### 5) Terraforming: How Ceiling Gains Become Real

- You don’t just use tools—you reshape the environment so the right work is the default.
- Preview: dive packs, guardrails, salvage loop, and memory.

## Concrete Takeaways

- One rule: protect the learning, not the artifact.
- One habit: require a one-sentence aim + 3–7 constraints *before* you generate.
- One metric: measure reversals, not output.
